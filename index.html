The problem

Customers are paying more than they need to — and don't know it

Our customers are on Time-of-Use tariffs where electricity prices vary significantly across the day. Most don't understand this, and almost none are actively shifting their usage to take advantage of cheaper windows.

The result: they consistently run high-consumption appliances — EV chargers, dishwashers, washing machines — during the most expensive hours of the day, often out of habit rather than necessity.

The approach

Rather than educating customers about tariff mechanics (confusing, jargon-heavy), we lead with the personal financial outcome: here's how much you could save, based on your own data.

An AI-powered guided tour overlays the customer's existing hourly usage chart — no new screens — and walks them through their specific patterns, explaining why certain hours cost more, and pointing at evidence in their own data to suggest 2–3 small timing changes.

The goal is behaviour change, but even customers who don't change will leave better informed. Education is the floor; savings are the ceiling.

Success metrics

We're measuring a four-stage funnel: hook rate (badge taps ÷ impressions) → completion rate (guide completions ÷ starts) → commit rate (reminders set ÷ completions) → behaviour change (sustained peak reduction vs control group over 6–8 weeks).

What to look for in the prototype

Tap "Save money" in the app header to start the guided tour. The chart animates to highlight each pricing window in turn, with annotations pointing directly at your usage patterns. The tour ends with 3 personalised suggestions — each with a single "Do it" button that sets a reminder and commits in one tap.

Key questions for reviewers: Does the hook feel credible? Does the chart annotation make the pricing difference visceral? Do the suggestions feel personal or generic?
